# Documentation Hub - User Manual

Welcome to Documentation Hub (also called **DocHub**)! This manual walks you
through everything the app does, in plain steps you can follow. No technical
knowledge is needed — if you can open a Word document, you can use DocHub.

DocHub is a desktop app that cleans up and standardizes Microsoft Word
documents for you. Instead of fixing spacing, lists, tables, headings, and
links by hand, you hand your documents to DocHub and it does the tidy-up work
in seconds — while always keeping a safe copy of your originals.

This manual is a companion to **What's New in Documentation Hub**, which covers
recent improvements and two specific features (Nuxeo Feedback and Document
Managers) in detail. Where those two features come up here, this manual keeps
things brief and points you back to that document.

---

## 1. Getting Started

### What the app is for

DocHub takes Word documents (the `.docx` kind) and applies a consistent set of
clean-up steps to them — fixing formatting, spacing, lists, tables, and links so
your finished documents all look the same and follow the same standards. You
choose what gets cleaned up, and the app keeps a backup of every original.

### The main window

When you open DocHub you'll see two main areas:

- **The sidebar** runs down the left edge. It's how you move between the
  different screens of the app.
- **The main area** fills the rest of the window and shows whichever screen you
  picked from the sidebar.

### The sidebar and navigation

The sidebar is grouped into sections:

- **Dashboard** — the home screen. Any sessions you currently have open appear
  listed just beneath it.
- **Workspace** — this group holds **Sessions**, **Analytics**, **Documents**,
  and **Reporting**.
- **Quick Links** — this group holds **Nuxeo Feedback** and **Document
  Managers**, plus any web links you've saved under them.
- **Settings** — found at the very bottom of the sidebar.

To go to a screen, just click its name in the sidebar. The screen you're
currently on is highlighted.

You can make the sidebar narrower to give yourself more room: click the small
round arrow button on the sidebar's edge, or press **Ctrl+Q**. Click it again
to bring the sidebar back to full width. When narrowed, hovering over an icon
shows you its name.

Most main screens also have a keyboard shortcut shown next to them in the
sidebar (for example, Dashboard, Sessions, Analytics, Documents, and
Reporting), so you can jump straight to them without clicking.

## 2. The Dashboard

The Dashboard is your home base and the first thing you see. It gives you a
quick overview of your work.

### What it shows

- **Summary cards** across the top show totals at a glance — how many documents
  you've processed, how many links have been checked, how much feedback has been
  imported, and how much time you've saved. Each card also shows whether today's
  numbers are up or down compared to yesterday.
- **Recent Sessions** lists the sessions you've worked on most recently. Each
  entry shows the session's name, how many documents it holds, and when it was
  last touched. Click any session in this list to open it again.

### Starting work from the Dashboard

In the top-right corner you'll find two buttons:

- **New Session** — start a fresh batch of documents to work on.
- **Load Session** — pick up an earlier session and continue where you left off.

If you've never created a session, the Dashboard simply invites you to create
your first one.

## 3. Adding and Processing Documents

This is the heart of the app: bringing Word documents in and letting DocHub
clean them up.

### Step 1: Start or open a session

Click **New Session** (from the Dashboard or the Sessions screen). Give your
session a short, friendly name — something that describes the batch of
documents you're about to work on — then confirm. The app takes you straight
into that session.

### Step 2: Add your documents

Inside a session you can add Word documents in two ways:

1. **Drag and drop** — drag one or more `.docx` files from your computer and
   drop them anywhere onto the session screen. A highlighted drop area appears
   to show the app is ready to receive them.
2. **Browse for files** — click **Load Files** (or **Add More Files** if you
   already have some) and pick your documents using the file picker.

Only Word `.docx` files are accepted. As documents are added they appear in a
list, newest at the top.

### Step 3: Choose what processing to do (optional)

By default DocHub applies a sensible, full set of clean-up steps. If you'd like
to fine-tune that, open the **Processing Options** tab inside the session
(more on this in Section 4). You can leave everything as-is if you're happy
with the standard clean-up.

### Step 4: Process the documents

You have two choices:

- Click **Process** next to a single document to run just that one.
- Click **Process Documents** at the top of the session to run every document
  that's waiting.

DocHub processes documents one at a time. While a batch is running, a banner
shows which document is being worked on and roughly how much time is left. You
can click **Cancel All** to stop the remaining documents.

Each document shows its status as it goes: waiting, processing, completed (with
a green check), or — if something went wrong — an error. When a document
finishes successfully, a short confirmation message appears.

### Step 5: Open or export your results

Once a document is **completed**, hover over it in the list to reveal extra
buttons:

- Open the finished document in Word.
- Open the backup copy of the original.
- Compare the before and after versions side by side (see Section 5).
- Show the document's location in a folder.

To save all finished documents somewhere together, click **Export Processed
Files** and choose a destination folder.

## 4. Working with a Session

### What a session is

A **session** is simply a named batch of documents you're working on together.
It keeps your documents, your processing choices, and your results grouped in
one place, so you can step away and come back later without losing anything.

A session inside the app is organized into tabs:

- **Session** — the main view, with your document list and the buttons to add,
  process, and export documents.
- **Processing Options** — choose exactly which clean-up steps to run.
- **Styles** — set your preferred fonts, headings, table shading, and similar
  formatting choices.
- **Replacements** — set up text that should be automatically swapped out
  during processing.
- **Document Changes** — review everything that was changed (see Section 5).

### Following a session's progress

The **Session** tab shows summary cards for that session — documents processed,
links checked, feedback imported, and time saved — so you can see at a glance
how the batch is going. Each document in the list shows its own status.

### What processing does for you

When you process a document, DocHub tidies it up so it looks consistent and
follows your standards. Depending on the options you've enabled, it can:

- **Tidy text formatting** — make text styling consistent, remove unwanted
  italics, and fix dashes and spacing characters.
- **Fix links and navigation** — repair broken internal links, keep the table
  of contents links working, and update "back to top" links.
- **Clean up document structure** — even out spacing and blank lines, remove
  stray whitespace, center and border images, and tidy headers and footers.
- **Standardize lists and tables** — make bullets and numbering consistent, fix
  list indentation, and apply uniform table styling and borders.

In the **Processing Options** tab the steps are grouped into clear categories.
You can turn whole groups on or off, or pick individual steps. A panel on the
right shows a plain-language description of every step you've enabled, so you
always know what will happen.

You don't have to set this up every time. Use **Save as Default** to remember
your current choices for future sessions, or **Reset** to return to the
standard settings.

## 5. Reviewing Changes

After processing, you'll usually want to see exactly what DocHub did. There are
two ways to do this.

### See a list of changes

Open the **Document Changes** tab inside the session. This shows a clear,
itemized list of every change made to your documents — what was changed and
where. You can filter the list to focus on a particular kind of change. A quick
shortcut: in the document list, double-click any completed document to jump
straight to its changes.

### Compare the before and after, side by side

To see the original and the processed version next to each other, hover over a
completed document and click the **Compare** button. DocHub opens both versions
in Word, positioned side by side so you can scroll through and check the
differences yourself.

If you use more than one monitor, you can choose which monitor the comparison
opens on from the **Display** group in Settings.

## 6. Sessions, Documents, and Search

DocHub gives you several ways to find past work.

### The Sessions screen

Open **Sessions** from the sidebar to see every session you've created. Each
session appears as a card showing its name, how many documents it has, and when
it was created and last changed. You can:

- **Search** for a session by name using the search box at the top.
- Switch between a grid layout and a list layout.
- Click a session to open it again.
- Delete a session you no longer need (you'll be asked to confirm — deleting
  cannot be undone).
- Start a **New Session** with the button in the corner.

### The Documents screen

Open **Documents** from the sidebar to see every document from every session in
one place. The top of the screen shows totals — how many documents in all, and
how many are completed, still waiting, or had an error. You can:

- Search for a document by its name or by its session.
- Filter the list by status (All, Completed, Pending, Errors).
- Open a finished document in Word, or open its folder location.

### Searching for documents

DocHub also offers a dedicated search experience for finding a specific
document quickly. Type part of a document's name, session, or location and
DocHub finds close matches even if you don't type it exactly. You can narrow
results by status and by session. Use the up and down arrow keys to move
through the results and press Enter to open the one you want.

## 7. Projects

The **Projects** screen gives you a place to view work organized into
larger projects, each shown as a card with a name, description, progress bar,
and team information. You can search projects and switch between grid and list
layouts. If you don't use projects, you can simply skip this screen — sessions
are all you need for processing documents.

## 8. Analytics

Open **Analytics** from the sidebar to see insights about your work over time.

The top of the screen shows your all-time totals — documents processed, links
checked, feedback imported, and time saved. Below that are charts showing how
those numbers have changed:

- **Documents Processed Over Time**
- **Hyperlinks Checked Over Time**
- **Activity Breakdown** — comparing several measures side by side.

You can switch the charts between a **Daily**, **Weekly**, or **Monthly** view.
Hover your mouse over any point on a chart to see the exact numbers for that
day, week, or month near the top of the screen.

If you ever want to start your statistics fresh, the **Reset Stats** button
clears all analytics history. You'll be asked to confirm first, because this
cannot be undone.

## 9. Settings

Open **Settings** from the bottom of the sidebar. Settings are organized into
groups, listed down the side of the screen — click a group to view it. You can
also search the settings using the search box. Here's a high-level tour:

- **Profile** — your personal information.
- **Appearance** — choose a light, dark, or system theme, pick an accent color,
  and adjust how spacious or compact the app feels.
- **Typography** — choose fonts and text styling for the app.
- **Display** — choose which monitor the side-by-side document comparison
  opens on.
- **Language** — region and language preferences.
- **Updates** — see your current app version and manage app updates.
- **API Connections** — connection details the app uses for link processing
  and for feedback and reporting.
- **Local Dictionary** — a word list the app can use while processing.
- **Submit Idea for New Implementation** — a place to send in suggestions for
  new features.
- **Backups** — turn automatic backups on or off and read how backups are
  named and stored (see Section 10).
- **Quick Links** — this group holds **Nuxeo Feedback** and **Document
  Managers**, where you save your own collection of web links.

After making changes in most settings groups, click **Save Settings** to keep
them. A confirmation appears on the button when the save succeeds.

## 10. Backups and Safety

DocHub is built to protect your original documents. Before it applies any
changes, it automatically saves a copy of the original.

- **Where backups go** — backups are stored in a folder named `DocHub_Backups`,
  created in your Downloads folder.
- **How backups are named** — each backup keeps the original file's name with a
  numbered tag added, so you build up a numbered history of versions and can
  always go back to an earlier one.
- **Staying in control** — automatic backups can be turned on or off in the
  **Backups** group in Settings. They're on by default, and it's best to leave
  them on unless you already have your own backup system.

Because of this, processing a document is a safe thing to do — your original is
never lost.

## 11. Tips and Troubleshooting

A few general pointers to keep things running smoothly:

- **Close the document in Word first.** If a Word document is open in Word while
  you try to process it, processing can't complete. Close it, then click
  **Retry** on that document.
- **One document had an error?** Click the error message next to the document
  to see details. Many errors can be fixed and retried — for example, closing
  the file or converting an older document in Word first.
- **Documents process one at a time.** If you add a large batch, that's normal —
  watch the progress banner for how much time is left, and use **Cancel All** if
  you need to stop.
- **Your work is saved automatically.** Sessions, settings, and processing
  choices are remembered, so you can close the app and pick up later.
- **Use Save as Default** for processing options and styles you use often, so
  you don't have to set them up for each new session.
- **Lost a document?** Use the **Documents** screen or the search experience to
  find it across all your sessions.
- **Something doesn't look right after processing?** Use **Document Changes** or
  the side-by-side **Compare** to review exactly what changed — and remember
  your original is always safe in the backups folder.
- **Need to send something in for help or feedback?** The **Reporting** screen
  packages up selected documents and opens a pre-filled email for you; the
  **Nuxeo Feedback** quick links give you fast access to feedback pages.

That's the whole app. With sessions to organize your work, processing to do the
clean-up, and backups to keep your originals safe, DocHub takes the repetitive
formatting work off your hands so you can focus on the content.
